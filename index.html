I use satellite and other observational data to study land–atmosphere interactions, focusing on how forest cover change influences clouds, radiation, and precipitation in a changing climate.

About Me

I am Tom Dror (she/her/hers), a postdoctoral researcher at CIRES, University of Colorado Boulder, embedded in the NOAA Chemical Sciences Laboratory, working in the Clouds, Aerosol, and Climate Group. My passion for clouds drives my research, which focuses on the coupling between land and clouds, largely in the tropics and the Amazon forest. For these investigations, my primary tools are measurements of the Earth's atmosphere, especially those from satellites.
