I use satellite and other observational data to study land–atmosphere interactions, focusing on how forest cover changes influence clouds, radiation, and precipitation in a changing climate.

About Me

I am Tom Dror (she/her/hers), a postdoctoral researcher for CIRES, University of Colorado Boulder, embedded in the NOAA Chemical Sciences Laboratory, working in the Clouds, Aerosol, and Climate Group. My curiosity about clouds drives my research, which explores the coupling of land and clouds—largely focused on the tropics and the Amazon forest. For these investigations, my primary tools of choice are measurements (or observations) of the Earth's atmosphere, especially those made from satellites.
